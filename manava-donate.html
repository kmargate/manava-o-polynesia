Mānava O Polynesia — Internal reference — Not a public page
Phase 1
Legal Requirements
Must complete before the donate button goes live. Adding an interactive donate button to a website constitutes a charitable solicitation under state law. Colorado requires registration before any solicitation occurs.
1

Required Confirm 501(c)(3) status is active

Verify the IRS determination letter is on file. Donors can only claim tax deductions for gifts to recognized 501(c)(3) organizations. You will need this letter for state registration.

Check status: IRS Tax Exempt Organization Search

2

Required Register with the Colorado Secretary of State

Every charitable organization soliciting donations from Colorado residents must register before soliciting — including via a website donate button.

Cost: $10 filing fee, renewed annually
Renewal deadline: 15th day of the 5th month after your fiscal year ends
Required documents: IRS determination letter, most recent Form 990, list of officers and directors, EIN

Step 2a — Check if already registered (charity search):
Search by org name at coloradosos.gov/ccsa/pages/search/basic.xhtml. If the org appears with an active status, skip to Step 2d (renew if needed).

Step 2b — Confirm business entity is registered:
The org must be registered as a nonprofit corporation before filing for charitable solicitation. Look up the business entity at sos.state.co.us/biz/BusinessEntityCriteriaExt.do. Search "Manava" or "Polynesia" and confirm it shows "Good Standing."

Step 2c — Create a Colorado SOS account and file:
Create a user account and file the charitable organization registration at sos.state.co.us/ccsa. All filings are done online. You will need up to 3 NTEE codes describing your programs — recommended codes for Mānava: A60 (Folk Arts), A80 (Cultural/Ethnic Awareness).

Step 2d — Renew annually:
Registration must be renewed each year by the 15th day of the 5th month after your fiscal year ends (e.g., fiscal year ending Dec 31 → renew by May 15).

Questions: 303-894-2200 option 2 — charitable@coloradosos.gov

3

Required Set up donation acknowledgment process

The IRS requires written acknowledgment for any single donation of $250 or more. This letter must state the amount donated, the date, and confirm no goods or services were provided in exchange.

Donorbox sends automated receipts to donors after each transaction — configure the receipt email in your campaign settings to meet this requirement.

4

Optional Multi-state registration (future)

If the site begins receiving donations from out-of-state donors on a recurring basis (more than $25,000/year or more than 50 donations from a single state), registration in that state is required. For now, Colorado registration is sufficient.

Reference: National Council of Nonprofits — Charitable Solicitation Registration

Phase 2
Donorbox Account & Campaign Setup
Payment processor note: You cannot accept donations directly into a bank account — a payment processor is required. Donorbox supports Stripe and PayPal. Stripe is recommended: it supports credit/debit cards, ACH bank transfers, Apple Pay, and Google Pay, and auto-deposits to your bank every 2 business days.
Processor Per-transaction fee (nonprofit rate) Transfer to bank
Stripe 2.2% + $0.30 Auto every 2 business days
PayPal 1.99% + $0.49 Manual — you initiate

Donorbox adds a 1.5% platform fee (donors are prompted to cover it — most do). No monthly or setup fee at the starter tier.

1

Create a free Donorbox account

Go to donorbox.org, select "Nonprofit," and enter your EIN. Donorbox will pull your organization's details automatically from the IRS database.

2

Connect Stripe

Inside Donorbox, go to Settings → Payment Gateways and connect Stripe. Donorbox walks you through opening a Stripe account (or linking an existing one) and connecting your bank account for deposits.

Stripe will require basic business verification: EIN, bank account details, and a representative's identity info.

3

Create a campaign

In Donorbox, create a new campaign (e.g., "Support Mānava O Polynesia"). Configure suggested donation amounts, a campaign description, and your receipt email. Publish the campaign.

Copy your campaign URL slug — it will look like: https://donorbox.org/manava-o-polynesia

4

Configure receipt email

In campaign settings, customize the automated receipt email. Include the org name, 501(c)(3) status statement, and confirmation that no goods or services were exchanged — this satisfies IRS acknowledgment requirements for gifts of $250+.

Phase 3
Code Changes — index.html
There are two donate elements in the site. Neither is currently functional. Both will be wired to the Donorbox popup — clicking them opens a polished donation form overlay without leaving the page.
1

Add Donorbox popup script to <head>

Add the following two lines before the closing </head> tag. Replace YOUR-CAMPAIGN-SLUG with your actual Donorbox campaign URL slug.

<script src="https://donorbox.org/install-popup-button.js" type="text/javascript" defer></script>
<script>window.DonorBox = { widgetLinkClassName: 'custom-dbox-popup' }</script>
2

Wire up the nav "Donate" link — line 1172

The existing <a class="nav-donate"> element needs an href and the Donorbox popup class added.

Before:

<a class="nav-donate">Donate</a>

After:

<a class="nav-donate custom-dbox-popup" href="https://donorbox.org/YOUR-CAMPAIGN-SLUG">Donate</a>
3

Wire up the "Donate to our mission" button — line 1521

The existing <button class="donate-btn"> has no action handler. Wrap it in an anchor tag to trigger the Donorbox popup.

Before:

<button class="donate-btn">Donate to our mission</button>

After:

<a href="https://donorbox.org/YOUR-CAMPAIGN-SLUG" class="custom-dbox-popup" style="display:block;">
  <button class="donate-btn">Donate to our mission</button>
</a>
Phase 4
Verification & Go-Live
1

Test locally before deploying

Open index.html in a browser and click both donate elements. The Donorbox popup form should open as an overlay. Confirm both the nav link and the "Donate to our mission" button trigger it.

2

Make a test donation

Use Stripe's test card (4242 4242 4242 4242, any future expiry, any CVV) to complete a test donation. Verify it appears in the Donorbox dashboard and in Stripe.

3

Confirm receipt email

Make a test donation using a real email address. Verify the automated receipt email arrives and contains the required IRS acknowledgment language.

4

Deploy to live site

Commit changes on dev, merge to main, and push. The Colorado SOS registration should be complete before this step.
